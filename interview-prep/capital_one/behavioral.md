## Leadership
1. Tell me about a time you showed leadership

Situation: Although I haven't had a formal leadership role, I had the opportunity to help onboard a new developer. One of her first tasks involved a feature that I owned.

Task: Since she was new to both the codebase and the team, I wanted to help her become productive without taking over the work myself.

Action: I walked her through how the feature worked, explained the architecture and the relevant parts of the code, and helped her break the work into smaller, manageable tasks. I also encouraged her to reach out whenever she got stuck, and throughout the task I answered her questions and helped clarify technical details.

Result: She completed the feature much faster than I expected for someone who was new to the team and had limited experience. It reinforced for me that leadership isn't just about managing people—it's also about enabling others to succeed by sharing knowledge and providing guidance.
